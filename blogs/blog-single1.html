  • Sign and submit the application.

    2). No : Take a printout and post it to DGFT
What is Import export Code and who needs it?

Taking your product and service to international market will definetely going to boost your business. But visualizing it can be difficult for uninitiated person. His/her job becomes more difficult because of various legal procedures that intenational trade has.

The first step in starting an Import Export Business is to obtain a IEC from DGFT(Directorate General of Foreign Trade). An IEC is a must for the import/export of the goods. For the import/export of the services IEC is required for the limited circumstances, such as technology sharing.

IEC code may not be required for the circumstances:

1). Import/export by government ministeries and departments, and certain notified charitable organizations.

2). Import/export of goods for personal use, which is not connected with trade, manufacture or agriculture.

Any bonafide person/ company starting a venture for International trade of Export and Import. If any foreign transfer on account of business, IEC number is mandatory.

What is IE Code number used in Export Import Business?

IEC stands for IMPORT EXPORT CODE which contains 10 digit number issued by DGFT(Directorate General of Foreign Trade), Department od Commerce, Government of India.

How to apply for an Import Export Code?

If DGFT is close to your place it is better to approach the DGFT personally. They will provide you excellent information about the documents and charges applicable for issuing of IEC. Other option is to applying for IEC online.

What are the documents required for issuing of Import Export Code application?

Document are listed below (Please reconfirm with the authorities, as these information can be changed amended time to time)
1). Certificate from the Banker of the applicant firm.
2). Copy of Permanent Account Number (PAN) issued by Income Tax Authorities.
3). Three copies of passport size photographs of the applicant.
4). Address proof of the applicant firm / individual.
5). Details of Partners / Director / Proprietor / Trustee & there complete details, List of branches, Regd off, Head office, factory located in India & abroad with contact details.
6). (a) In case of Limited companies (both Private and Public Ltd.):
1). Extract of Board Resolution in favour of the applicant.
2). Memorandum of Association along with Certificate of Incorporation.
3). Form 32 in case of change of Directors and Form 18 in case of change of Registered office-wherever applicable.
(b) In case of Partnership firm:
1). Notarized Partnership Deed showing date of formation of the firm.
2).No Objection Certificate from other Partners/HUF.
