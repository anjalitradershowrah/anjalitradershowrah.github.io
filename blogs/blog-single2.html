Exporting As An Entrepreneur - Steps.

RahulSah 12:49 AM Exporting As An Entrepreneur - Steps
In order to succeed in the era of globalization, it makes sense for entrepreneurs to plan for export/import business as part of their business strategy. There are various good initivates taken by Government of India to ease the process of exporting. Recently government of India had started various schemes under ambitious "Startup India, StandUp India".

Various incentives are been provided for young Entrepreneur and specially Women Entrepreneurs. Young entrepreneurs planning for startup global trade should be aware of the steps involved in export business and international trade. These steps are listed below.

  • Step 1 : Selecting the Right Name

    Words like 'International' or 'Overseas' in the name of the firm (e.g., AWEST International, AWEST overseas, Rahul International Traders, International Export Ltd) convey the message that the firm is engaged in export/import business and international trade.

  • Step 2: Registration

    The registration of the organization(partnership or sole proprietorship) is another important step. The firm has to be registered under the country's pravelent law such as Companies Act.

  • Step 3: Opening a Bank Acoount

    The registrated firm need to open an account with a bank dealing in foreign exchange. It make sense to open an acoount with a branch which directly undertakes import export document and foreign currency exchange.

  • Step 4: Quoting PAN(Permanent Account Number)

    The PAN needs to be quoted to apply for the import export code(IEC) number and to claim tax exemptions and deduction under IT Act Details.

  • Step 5: Registering in Sales Tax Officer

    The exporter need not pay sales tax while making purchase for export. To avail this facilities Business Startup firm needs to be registered with Sale Tax Office.

  • Step 6: Obtaining IEC Number

    IEC Code is the most important registration requirement for an exporter/importer. No export or import for commercial purpose can be made any person without IEC Number. An IEC Code can be obtained by applying to relvant authority i.e; DGFT in India. This is no expiry date for the IEC Code.
    The application should be accompained by relevant documents, which are as follows:
    • Application form (in duplicate)
    • Company profile (in duplicate)
    • True copy of imcome tax PAN(Permanent Account Number)
    • True copy of sales tax certificate, if any
    • Government Fees
    • Bank Certificate as per format
    • Full address of the branches in India and abroad, if any
    • Three passport sized photographs duly signed on the reverse.
    • SSI registration copy, duly certified, if any
    • Declarations in duplicate
    • `

  • Step 7: Registeration with Export Promotion Council

    Government is many country have set up Export Promotion Council(EPC) to provide information and to facilitate exports.

    The basic objective of EPC is to promote and develop the exports of the country. There are various council in EPC each is dedicated a particular group of products, projects and services. The EPCs keep abreast of the latest trends and opportunites in international markets for goods and services ans assist their members in taking advantage of such oppurunities in orer to expand and diversify exports.

  • Step 8: Registeration with Export Credit Guarantee Corporation

    Exporters should register with the Export Credit Guarantee Corporation(ECGC) to secure payment against political and commercial risk.

  • Step 9: Central Excercise

    Goods are subject to exemption from excise duty on the final product meant for export. Where exemption is not availed, the excise duty is refunded after actual export. Moreover, the refund of the excise duty is made on inputs used in the manufacture of goods meant for export. Form ARE-1 has to be used in India for the excise clearance.

  • Step 10: Registration with the Chambers of Commerce, Productivity Councils, etc

    It is desirable for a firm to become a member of the local chamber of commerce, productivity council,l or trade promotion organization recongized by the Ministry of Commerce. This is helpful in many ways, including obtaining the Certificate of Origin

  • Step 11: Registration of Business Indentification Number

    It is important to obtain the PAN, based on the Business Indentification Number (BIN), from the DGFT, registration office prior to filing for custom clearance of export goods.

  • Step 12: Export Licence, if required

    Many items are free for export, without any licence, if they do not in the negative list. the negative list can consist of iemsm that are:

    • Prohibited(i.e; cannot be exported)
    • Restricted(i.e; throughm licence)

    An exporter has to procure a licence, if his item is not listed under the freely exportable items by the government.
